What is Electrolysis?
Electrolysis, process by which electric current is passed through a substance to effect a chemical change. The chemical change is one in which the substance loses or gains an electron (oxidation or reduction). The process is carried out in an electrolytic cell, an apparatus consisting of positive and negative electrodes held apart and dipped into a solution containing positively and negatively charged ions

key terms...
  1. Electrode is a rod of metal or graphite through which an electric current flows into or out of an electrolyte
  2. Electrolyte is the ionic compound in molten or dissolved solution that conducts the electricity
  3. Anode is the positive electrode of an electrolysis cell
  4. Anion is a negatively charged ion which is attracted to the anode
  5. Cathode is the negative electrode of an electrolysis cell
  6. Cation is a positively charged ion which is attracted to the cathode
